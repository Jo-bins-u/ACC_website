A Heart ofPrayer

Why We Pray

Prayer is a fundamental way to connect with God. It serves as a communication of heart, expressing gratitude, seeking guidance, and building a personal relationship with our faith. Through prayer, we find comfort and strength, following the teachings of Jesus who himself prayed frequently.

Common Prayers

Our Father
Our Father, Who art in heaven, Hallowed be Thy Name. Thy Kingdom come. Thy Will be done, on earth as it is in Heaven.

Give us this day our daily bread. And forgive us our trespasses, as we forgive those who trespass against us. And lead us not into temptation, but deliver us from evil. Amen.
Hail Mary
Hail Mary, Full of Grace, The Lord is with thee. Blessed art thou among women, and blessed is the fruit of thy womb, Jesus.

Holy Mary, Mother of God, pray for us sinners now, and at the hour of our death. Amen.
Glory Be
Glory be to the Father, and to the Son, and to the Holy Spirit.

As it was in the beginning, is now, and ever shall be, world without end. Amen.

(Holy Wednesday evening to Holy Saturday evening)

Alleluia.

Alleluia.

Has risen, as he said,
Alleluia.

Rejoice and be glad, O virgin Mary,
Alleluia.

Because the Lord is truly risen,
Alleluia.

Let us pray: O God, who by the Resurrection of your Son, our Lord Jesus Christ, granted joy to the whole world, grant, we beseech you, that through the intercession of the Virgin Mary, his Mother, we may enjoy the happiness of eternal life, through the same Christ Our Lord. Amen.

The Rosary

Opening Prayers
In the name of the Father, and of the Son, and of the Holy Spirit. Amen.

O Mary Immaculate, our mother, please accept the recitation of this rosary as an expression of our filial love and devotion towards you. May it be to us an incentive to purity and an encouragement to walk along the path of faithful devotion to our duty.

The Apostle's Creed I believe in God, the Father Almighty, Creator of heaven and earth, and in Jesus Christ, His only Son, our Lord, who was conceived by the Holy Spirit, born of the Virgin Mary, suffered under Pontius Pilate, was crucified, died and was buried; He descended into hell; on the third day He rose again from the dead; He ascended into heaven, and is seated at the right hand of God the Father Almighty; from there He will come to judge the living and the dead. I believe in the Holy Spirit, the Holy Catholic Church, the communion of Saints, the forgiveness of sins, the resurrection of the body, and life everlasting. Amen.

The Mysteries

Joyful Mysteries (Monday & Saturday)
1. The Annunciation Contemplate the humility of Mary and her conformity to God's will. (1 Our Father, 10 Hail Marys, 1 Glory Be) Fatima Prayer: O my Jesus, forgive us our sins, save us from the fire of hell, lead all souls into heaven, especially those who are in most need of your mercy.

2. The Visitation Ask for the spirit of readiness to be of service. (1 Our Father, 10 Hail Marys, 1 Glory Be) Fatima Prayer...

3. The Birth of Jesus Let us learn from the stable spirit of poverty and simplicity of life. (1 Our Father, 10 Hail Marys, 1 Glory Be) Fatima Prayer...

4. The Presentation of Jesus Obedience to God's law and loving acceptance of necessary customs. (1 Our Father, 10 Hail Marys, 1 Glory Be) Fatima Prayer...

5. Finding Jesus in the Temple Pray for fidelity to our vocation. (1 Our Father, 10 Hail Marys, 1 Glory Be, 1 Fatima Prayer)

Proceed to Litany & Concluding Prayers
Sorrowful Mysteries (Tuesday & Friday)
1. The Agony in the Garden Ask for fervor in prayer. (1 Our Father, 10 Hail Marys, 1 Glory Be, 1 Fatima Prayer)
2. The Scourging at the Pillar The sight of Christ bathed in blood makes pleasure lose all its attraction. (1 Our Father, 10 Hail Marys, 1 Glory Be, 1 Fatima Prayer)
3. The Crowning with Thorns Purity of mind and meekness of heart. (1 Our Father, 10 Hail Marys, 1 Glory Be, 1 Fatima Prayer)
4. The Carrying of the Cross Every follower of Christ must be a cross bearer. (1 Our Father, 10 Hail Marys, 1 Glory Be, 1 Fatima Prayer)
5. The Crucifixion Jesus dies for our sins. (1 Our Father, 10 Hail Marys, 1 Glory Be, 1 Fatima Prayer)

Proceed to Litany & Concluding Prayers
Glorious Mysteries (Wednesday & Sunday)
1. The Resurrection If risen with Christ, live the new life in Christ. (1 Our Father, 10 Hail Marys, 1 Glory Be, 1 Fatima Prayer)
2. The Ascension Go and teach all nations by word and example. (1 Our Father, 10 Hail Marys, 1 Glory Be, 1 Fatima Prayer)
3. The Descent of the Holy Spirit We become the children of God when our life is permeated with his Spirit. (1 Our Father, 10 Hail Marys, 1 Glory Be, 1 Fatima Prayer)
4. The Assumption of Mary A holy life is the best guarantee for a happy death. (1 Our Father, 10 Hail Marys, 1 Glory Be, 1 Fatima Prayer)
5. The Coronation of Mary Bliss in heaven with Jesus and Mary. (1 Our Father, 10 Hail Marys, 1 Glory Be, 1 Fatima Prayer)

Proceed to Litany & Concluding Prayers
Luminous Mysteries (Thursday)
1. The Baptism of Jesus at Jordan Baptism of the Spirit is the first step in Christian discipleship. (1 Our Father, 10 Hail Marys, 1 Glory Be, 1 Fatima Prayer)
2. The Miracle at the Wedding of Cana Let's learn to call upon Jesus at every moment in life. (1 Our Father, 10 Hail Marys, 1 Glory Be, 1 Fatima Prayer)
3. Proclamation of the Kingdom Radical Christian discipleship is a call to constant conversion. (1 Our Father, 10 Hail Marys, 1 Glory Be, 1 Fatima Prayer)
4. The Transfiguration Let's strive to be transformed by partaking in the glory of Jesus. (1 Our Father, 10 Hail Marys, 1 Glory Be, 1 Fatima Prayer)
5. Institution of the Eucharist Daily Eucharist renews Christian commitment to unconditional sharing. (1 Our Father, 10 Hail Marys, 1 Glory Be, 1 Fatima Prayer)

Hail Holy Queen
Hail Holy Queen, Mother of mercy! Hail, our life, our sweetness and our hope! To you do we cry, poor banished children of Eve; to you do we send up our sighs, mourning and weeping in this valley of tears! Turn then, most gracious advocate, your eyes of mercy toward us; and after this, our exile, show unto us the blessed fruit of your womb, Jesus. O clement, O loving, O sweet Virgin Mary! Amen.
Memorare
Remember, O most gracious Virgin Mary, that never was it known that anyone who fled to your protection, implored your help or sought your intercession, was left unaided. Inspired by this confidence, we fly unto you, O Virgin of virgins, my Mother; to you do we come, before you we stand, sinful and sorrowful; O Mother of the Word Incarnate, despise not our petitions, but in your mercy hear and answer them. Amen.
Prayer for the Souls in Purgatory
Eternal rest grant unto them, O Lord, and let perpetual light shine upon them. May they rest in peace. Amen.
